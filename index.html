Welcome to FlyUC
Vertical flight at UC

FlyUC is a student organization at the College of Engineering and Applied Science, University of Cincinnati. The FlyUC team was initially formed for the GoFly Prize in 2017. FlyUC is a proud GoFly Phase III team. Today, FlyUC continues to work on designing and developing vertical take-off and landing aircrafts. The object of the organization is to create a platform for students interested in vertical flight to expand their knowledge and further develop the technology. The team is comprised of four sub-teams: Propulsion, Electronics, Structures, and Operations. We are open to all University of Cincinnati students, regardless of majors and colleges. 


  • 2017

    FlyUC Enters GoFly 

    FlyUC was born during 2017 to compete in Boeing's GoFly competition. The goal was to create a personal VTOL aircraft to navigate busy cities.

  • 2017-2019

    Struggles with Initial Design

    Throughout 2017-2019, FlyUC went through 3 design iterations to meet GoFly's rigours standards. However, all of these designs were deemed insufficient for a variety of reasons.

  • Summer 2019

    FlyUC leaves GoFly to Work Independently

    After several failed designs, the team voted to leave the GoFly Competition during the summer of 2019 to pursue the project on their own without the difficult timeline.

  • 2019-Present

    FlyUC Develops P1

    After leaving GoFly, FlyUC continued their work on the electric VTOL project through the creation of prototype 1, P1. A simple quadcopter design, P1 was created as a means to test complicated technology before moving to a larger design.
